The

Temple

The Tempietto del Fauno is located in the south-east corner of the garden of Palazzo Borromeo Arese. Arriving in Cesano Maderno, you can see its size from a distance, only partially shielded by trees, leaving even the unwitting traveler to prefigure the presence of a historical testimony the whose meaning extends far beyond its own architectural limits.

EXTERNAL


At the south east end of the park is the seventeenth-century building now called "Tempietto del Fauno", originally simply called "Casino at the bottom of the garden" ", since the statue of the god Pan, now located in the center of the hall on the ground floor, was formerly placed on a small hill in the middle of the nearby wood. Greek cross construction with rounded corners consisting of four open apses supported by columns tuscaniche architraved, is completed at the top by one spherical dome, externally supported by a lantern and surmounted by a statue.




INDOOR


Inside, the seventeenth-century frescoes show subjects related to time (the months and the zodiac) the nature (in the dome they can be identified although very damaged four seasons) and a probable reference to the rural culture of mythological derivation. Under the floor, a small four-leaf clover-shaped cellar was used for storage refreshments to be consumed in the park during the hot season.
